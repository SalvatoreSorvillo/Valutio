# Valutio Trademarks

"Valutio" (the word mark) and the Valutio logos and brand marks (together, the
"Trademarks") identify the official Valutio project, maintained by Salvatore
Sorvillo.

Open-source licenses grant **copyright** permissions: they let you use, modify,
and redistribute the code. **Trademarks are different.** This policy explains
what you may and may not do with the Trademarks. Its goal is simple: keep the
name "Valutio" reliably pointing to the official project, so users are never
misled about what they are running or who stands behind it.

The Trademarks are **not** licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License
or any other open-source license that covers the code.

## Allowed without permission

You may, without asking first:

1. **Refer to Valutio descriptively**, e.g. "compatible with Valutio", "based
   on Valutio", "a fork of Valutio", when the statement is accurate and does
   not imply that your project is official or endorsed.
2. Use the Trademarks in **articles, reviews, tutorials, screenshots, and
   documentation** that discuss Valutio.
3. **Redistribute unmodified official builds** of Valutio and use the Trademarks
   to identify them as the official version.
4. Use the Trademarks to **link to the official project** (its website or
   repository) in a way that does not imply endorsement.

## Not allowed without written permission

You may **not**, without prior written permission from the Maintainer:

1. Use "Valutio" (or a confusingly similar name) as the name of a **modified or
   forked version**, a competing product, a company, a domain name, or an app in
   an app store.
2. Use the Valutio **logo or brand marks as the icon or branding of your own
   distribution**, fork, or product.
3. Use the Trademarks in any way that **implies official status, sponsorship, or
   endorsement** by the Valutio project when none exists.

## If you distribute a modified version

The AGPL lets you modify and redistribute the code. Please do. But if you
distribute a modified version, you must **give it a different name** and use your
own branding, and make clear it is not the official Valutio. You may state,
accurately, that it is "based on Valutio."

## Questions and permission

To request permission, or if you are unsure whether a use is allowed, contact
**support@valutio.app**. We are happy to allow reasonable uses that don't confuse
users about what is official.
